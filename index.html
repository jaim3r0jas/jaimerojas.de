Jaime Rojas is a backend engineer and builder based in Munich, with 15+ years of experience turning hard problems into working software. By day he's a Senior Backend Engineer at SafeNow. On the side, he's building Famlo — an AI-native family operating system where chores, finances, meals, goals, and the calendar all flow from one place: how families actually talk to each other.

Now

By day at SafeNow, by night at Famlo.

Going full beast-mode on AI — learning how the new models, tools, and patterns let one builder ship what used to take a small team. Famlo is the lab; every prototype either compounds into the product or teaches why it shouldn't.

The why starts personal: a young family far from home, and no app that actually held daily life together. The bigger why follows — Famlo would have needed a team a year ago. Today, with AI as leverage, it's doable solo, and the families it's built for don't have to wait for someone else to build it.

The deeper bet: AI doesn't just speed up the work, it changes what software is allowed to feel like. Famlo's signature — communication at the root, modules flowing into each other naturally — only exists because AI can finally be the connective tissue, not a feature in a settings menu.

Famlo — Family Operating System

Built solo, driven by real life. No app actually held the family's life together after moving to Munich with twin 5-year-olds. Famlo is the answer — a family OS where communication is the root and everything else derives from it. Families talk, decisions follow, life happens. AI weaves the modules together so a complex platform feels like chatting with another member of the family.

  • Comms-first by design — chores, meals, finances, goals, and calendar all derive from how the family already talks
  • AI ties the modules together so the platform feels like a conversation, not an interface
  • Privacy-first — no trackers, no third-party data sharing
  • Waitlist beta, built entirely solo alongside full-time work
